⚡ PEM Electrolyzer Digital Twin

What is this?

This is a 3D interactive visualization of a Proton Exchange Membrane (PEM) electrolyzer stack. The digital twin simulates the physical hardware and calculates electrochemical physics in real-time across all 9 layers.

What you can do:

  • Rotate: Click and drag the 3D view to rotate
  • Zoom: Scroll to zoom in/out
  • Select layers: Click any layer to see detailed information
  • Adjust parameters: Use sliders to change current, temperature, and pressure
  • View heat maps: See spatial distributions of current density, temperature, and overpotentials
  • Compare states: Toggle between current and predicted operating conditions

The 9-layer stack structure:

H₂O Cavity → Water Feed Membrane → H₂ Cavity (cathode) → Cathode PTL → Cathode Catalyst → PEM → Anode Catalyst → Anode PTL → O₂ Cavity (anode)

📘 User Guide

Understanding the Visualization

The 3D stack shows the actual physical layers of a PEM electrolyzer. Heat maps on the catalyst layers display calculated values at each spatial location (10×10 grid). Colors indicate field intensity:

  • Blue: Lower values
  • Yellow: Medium values
  • Red: Higher values

Controls Reference

State Toggle: Switch between current operating conditions and predicted future state (with adjustable parameters).

Operating Parameters:

  • Current: Total stack current (5-40 A)
  • Temperature: Inlet water temperature (60-90 °C)
  • Pressure: Operating pressure (10-50 bar)

Heat Map Types:

  • Current Density: Local current per area (A/cm²)
  • Temperature: Local temperature with heat generation (°C)
  • Anode Activation: Energy barrier at anode (mV)
  • Cathode Activation: Energy barrier at cathode (mV)
  • Concentration: Mass transport losses (mV)
  • Total Overpotential: Sum of all voltage losses (mV)

View Controls

Reset Camera: Return to default viewing angle

Explode View: Separate layers to see internal structure

Toggle Transparency: Make flow fields transparent to see inside

Physics Model

The digital twin uses a 12-parameter hybrid physics model combining:

  • Nernst Equation: Thermodynamic reversible voltage
  • Butler-Volmer Kinetics: Electrochemical reaction rates
  • Ohmic Losses: Resistance in conductors and membrane
  • Fick's Law: Concentration overpotential from mass transport

Layer Descriptions

H₂O Inlet Cavity: Water distribution manifold (cyan, transparent)

Anode Flow Field: Channels for water flow and O₂ removal (dark gray)

Anode GDL/PTL: Porous transport layer for uniform distribution (darker gray)

Anode Catalyst Layer: Oxygen evolution reaction site (black, with heat map)

Membrane (PEM): Proton-conducting polymer (orange, semi-transparent)

Cathode Catalyst Layer: Hydrogen evolution reaction site (black, with heat map)

Cathode GDL/PTL: Porous transport layer (darker gray)

Cathode Flow Field: Channels for H₂ collection (dark gray)

O₂ Outlet Cavity: Gas collection manifold (light cyan, transparent)

Tips for Best Results

  • Use Explode View to understand the stack structure
  • Try different heat maps to see how parameters distribute spatially
  • Adjust operating conditions and observe how heat maps change
  • Click on catalyst layers to see detailed electrochemical data
